Regular grooming is about much more than aesthetics — it's a cornerstone of your pet's health and wellbeing. A clean coat, trimmed nails, and healthy ears all contribute to a happier, more comfortable animal.

## Brushing and Coat Care

How often you brush your pet depends on coat type:

- **Short-haired breeds** need brushing once a week
- **Medium-length coats** benefit from brushing 2–3 times a week
- **Long-haired breeds** may need daily brushing to prevent tangles

Regular brushing distributes natural oils, reduces shedding, and gives you the opportunity to check for lumps, parasites, or skin irritation.

## Bathing

Most dogs need a bath every 4–6 weeks, though this varies by breed and lifestyle. Always use a pet-safe shampoo — human shampoos can disrupt your pet's skin pH. Make sure to thoroughly rinse out all shampoo residue.

For cats, bathing is rarely necessary unless they've gotten into something. Cats are excellent self-groomers.

## Nail Trimming

Overgrown nails can cause discomfort, affect posture, and even lead to joint problems. Trim nails every 3–4 weeks, or visit your groomer or vet for this service if you're uncomfortable doing it yourself.

## Ear Cleaning

Check your pet's ears weekly for redness, discharge, or odour — all signs of infection. Clean ears gently with a vet-approved ear cleaner and cotton balls. Never insert anything into the ear canal.

## Dental Hygiene

Dental disease is the most common health issue in pets over age 3. Brush your pet's teeth several times a week with pet-safe toothpaste, and schedule annual professional cleanings with your vet.

## Professional Grooming

Consider professional grooming every 6–8 weeks for longer-coated breeds. A professional groomer can also spot issues you might miss, from skin conditions to early signs of ear problems.

Grooming is quality bonding time — approach it with patience and positive reinforcement for the best results.
